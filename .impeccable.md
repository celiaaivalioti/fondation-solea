## Design Context

### Users
French-speaking cancer patients and their families/loved ones. They arrive at this site while processing a difficult diagnosis or treatment journey — often fatigued, emotionally vulnerable, and possibly researching on a phone between appointments. Secondary audience: donors, medical partners (oncologists, care centers), and prospective committee/therapist collaborators.

### Brand Personality
Warm, human, hopeful, elegant, calm, non-clinical. A "high-quality Swiss foundation" register — credible and serious, but never cold or corporate. Should read as a trustworthy nonprofit with genuine care behind it, not a wellness brand and not a hospital.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Editorial, calm, spacious. Serif display type (Georgia) paired with a clean sans body (Inter), soft sage-green (#90aa7d) and cream palette, generous whitespace, full-bleed photography of real human moments (not stock-generic).
- Anti-references: hospital/medical-corporate design, generic NGO templates, trendy startup/SaaS landing pages (bold gradients, big sans-serif claims, product-tour scroll patterns).
- Theme: light only — appropriate for calm, daytime, informational browsing by patients/families.

### Design Principles
1. Never look clinical or corporate — no hospital blue, no sterile grids, no medical iconography.
2. Never look like a SaaS/startup landing page — no gradient hero claims, no feature-grid-with-icons pattern, no product-tour rhythm.
3. Respect emotional vulnerability: generous pacing, no urgency/pressure tactics, reassuring and unhurried copy and motion.
4. WCAG AA baseline: solid color contrast, readable type sizes, visible focus states, keyboard operability — some visitors are fatigued or unwell.
5. Photography and real human warmth carry the emotional weight; UI chrome should stay quiet and get out of the way.
